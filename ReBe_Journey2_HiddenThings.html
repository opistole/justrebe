The Hidden Things.
What Has Been Carried in Silence.

The average person carries 13 secrets at any given time — 5 of which have never been spoken aloud to another person. Not because they are shameful. Because a safe place has never existed to put them.

This journey creates that place. It will ask about what has been hidden, what has been used to cope, what has been inherited — and what it would mean to finally set it down.

Before You Begin
This journey touches real territory — secrets, shame cycles, behaviors that have felt impossible to name. Nothing here is stored. Nothing here is shared. What is brought into this space is held gently. There is no verdict. There is only the beginning of what becomes possible when something hidden is finally seen.
15 questions across the 5 RE stages — each one moving deeper into what has been hidden and what it costs to keep it there
The soul wound → trigger → soul pattern framework — so you can finally see where the behaviors are actually coming from
Nothing is labeled as yours. What has been present is not who you are. The distinction is everything.
Ends with an invitation to a room where none of this has to be carried alone any longer.

Stage 2 — Camera
The Reveal.
Seeing the pattern underneath the behavior.
Every behavior that has felt out of control, shameful, or confusing is a symptom — never the root. The next three questions begin to show the root. Soul wounds create triggers. Triggers activate soul patterns. The pattern is not who you are. It is what happened to you, showing up in disguise.
🔓
Stage 3 — Action
The Exposure.
What loses power when it is finally named.
A secret loses a significant part of its grip the moment it is named — even silently, to yourself, in a private space like this one. The next three questions invite naming. Not storytelling. Not shame. Just: this has been present. And I am done carrying it alone.
🌉
Stage 4 — Bridge
The Reflection.
From carrying it to seeing it clearly.
The next three questions invite the shift from inside the pattern to outside it. The shift from "this is who I am" to "this is what has been visiting." The bridge between a closed door and an open one begins right here.
🗝️
Stage 5 — Exit
The Declaration.
What is no more.
The final three questions move toward the door — naming what is done, choosing what comes next, and declaring — aloud if possible — what will no longer be carried. This is the RE to BE moment. Walk out with something true.
